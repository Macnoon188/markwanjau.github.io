Creating a Minimal, Techy Personal Website with TailwindCSS

Published: Monday, 9 March 2026 • Author: Mark Andrew Wanjau

I have always liked clean websites — the kind that feel simple on the outside but quietly say, “someone who understands technology built this.”

When I started thinking about a personal developer website, I did not want something loud, overly animated, or cluttered.

I wanted something minimal, structured, and slightly techy. Something that reflects how I work.

That is how I ended up building my site with TailwindCSS.

Why TailwindCSS?

When you are building a personal site, CSS can either be your best friend or the thing that slows you down.

I did not want to fight with CSS. I wanted to focus on layout, clarity, and readability.

Tailwind gave me three big wins:

✔ I could design directly in HTML without jumping across files
✔ Styling was predictable and consistent
✔ It naturally encouraged a clean, minimal aesthetic

Instead of global classes that try to do everything, Tailwind makes you think in small design decisions.

Padding. Spacing. Typography. Color contrast.

It feels like LEGOs for UI.

The Feel I Was Going For

I wanted the site to feel like this:

• Dark background
• Modern typography
• Soft neon accents
• Enough spacing to breathe
• Simple structure, no clutter

Not corporate. Not flashy. Not template-heavy.

Just clean, usable, and honest.

The site needed to introduce me, show my homelab work, my cybersecurity journey, and later my development projects.

I wanted it to feel like a personal lab corner on the internet — not a marketing page.

What I Actually Built

I structured the site around three ideas:

1️⃣ Who I am and what I am learning
2️⃣ The real hands-on work I am doing
3️⃣ A blog space to tell the story behind the work

No unnecessary pages. No filler content.

Tailwind made it easy to:

• Create reusable components
• Keep spacing consistent
• Make the site responsive without stress

When something did not look right, a small class tweak often fixed it immediately.

What I Learned Along the Way

Building your own website teaches you a lot even before you publish your first article.

I learned that:

• Simple is harder than it looks
• Design is about intention, not decoration
• Your website should tell your story, not just list skills

It felt good creating something that actually represents me instead of using a random template.

What’s Next

This website is not finished. It is growing with me.

As I continue learning cybersecurity, homelab engineering, and development, the site will evolve too.

I plan to:

• Add more blog posts
• Refine the UI
• Build interactive sections
• Showcase more real-world projects

For now, it does what I wanted. It gives me a home online.

If you are thinking about building your own personal site, TailwindCSS is worth trying.

Would you build yours with Tailwind — or choose something else?
